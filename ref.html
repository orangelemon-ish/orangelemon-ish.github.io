A Record of Now

Tenth Grade - Selected World Literature

Throughout my sophomore year, my English teacher had us complete a yearlong project reflecting on life in th midst of a pandemic. This reflection took the form of various kinds of writing, from formal to creative, as well as photography, poetry, and group discussions.

This reflection was a sort of catharsis, helping me process and come to terms with remote school, missed opportunities, and pandemic life. Over the course of the year, I was able to reflect on pandemic life and move beyond the negative to explore the importance of small things, focusing on the joy still present in the world.

Neurobiology Final Project - How Does the Brain Process Time?

January 2021 - Eleventh Grade - Neurobiology

To cap off my independent study in neurobiology, I chose to explore how time is processed in the human brain. This is a fairly under-researched area, and we don't yet fully understand how exactly people percieve the passage of time, and the fully neural pathways that allow them to do so. In the project, I started out with an exploration of biological clocks, like circadian rhythms and hippocampal time cells, and then moved into how the brain experiences the passage of time. I then focused on how time perception is altered during traumatic events, and specifically during the COVID-19 pandemic.

To create this project, I first had to research several topics in neurobiology, and choose one to focus on that I was not only interested in, but had enough of a body of research to allow me to create a thorough product. Additionally, this allowed me to develop my research skills, as I had to delve into research papers and articles on a relatively less well-documented topic and then synthesize bits and pieces of each source into the final product.
