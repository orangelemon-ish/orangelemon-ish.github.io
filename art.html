Adaptation of The Metamorphosis

This artifact is the three-episode adaptation of Franz Kafka’s classic, The Metamorphosis, which my AP Lang (Modern Euro) class created in junior year. It reflects the contemporary, multiliterate scholar attribute, since it required me to first read and analyze the novel in a more typical English-class way in order to understand its deeper meaning. I then had to be a screenwriter, working to stay true to the novel and its themes while also adding a new, original spin to it. Then, as both an actor and a crew member, I had to work with the rest of the class to film our saga in just two and a half classes, coordinating quick set changes and dealing with various mishaps quickly and effectively.
This artifact is important to me because it represents not only a chance to work with what we learned in a fun, creative manner, but also a really fun, hilarious time I got to work with my friends to create something vaguely ridiculous. Through the process of creating this adaptation, I gained a (albeit limited) knowledge of how movies and such are filmed, and the work that goes into them. Though I can’t say I’m particularly artistically talented, this relates to my desire to learn more about the artistic process and such.
